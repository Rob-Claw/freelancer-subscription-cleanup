# AI Health Prep Auditor — GTM v1

## Positioning
Do **not** sell this as an AI compliance platform.

Sell it as:
- **Opening shift readiness for small restaurants**
- **Photo-proof checklists for critical kitchen tasks**
- **Stop failing inspections for stupid preventable things**

## Best First ICP
Target:
- Independent restaurants
- Food trucks
- Small local chains
- 1–3 locations
- 5–30 staff
- No enterprise ops stack
- Running on paper, WhatsApp, verbal reminders, or inconsistent checklists

Avoid for now:
- Large chains
- Enterprise franchise groups
- Restaurants already deep in enterprise software

## Main Pain Angles
1. Managers assume critical prep tasks got done when they did not
2. Opening shift readiness is inconsistent
3. Paper checklists create fake accountability
4. Inspection-risk tasks get skipped when service pressure starts
5. Owners waste time manually verifying basics

## Core Offer
**"We’ll set up your restaurant’s opening checklist for you in 15 minutes."**

That is the offer, not just the software.

## Pilot Offer
- Free 7–14 day pilot
- One location
- We help configure the checklist
- Staff use it during real shifts
- Manager gets daily visibility

## Pricing Hypothesis
- Pilot: free
- Paid: **$19–39/month per location**
- Price anchor: cheaper than one stupid preventable inspection problem or repeated manager babysitting

## Cold Outreach Angles
### Angle 1 — Inspection risk
"Built a simple mobile checklist with photo proof for restaurants so managers can verify critical prep tasks before service. The goal is to prevent stupid misses that turn into inspection problems. Want to see a 2-minute demo?"

### Angle 2 — Manager visibility
"Quick one — I built a lightweight restaurant opening checklist tool with photo proof for critical tasks. It helps managers confirm what actually got done before the shift gets busy. Open to a fast demo?"

### Angle 3 — Paper checklist replacement
"If your team still uses paper opening checklists, I built a dead-simple mobile replacement with photo proof so it’s not just boxes getting ticked blindly. Want to try it for a week?"

## Sample DM / Cold Message
Hi [Name] — built a simple tool for restaurant opening/prep checklists with photo proof for critical tasks like sanitizer stations, fridge temp checks, and cleaning checks.

It’s meant for small restaurants that are still handling this with paper, WhatsApp, or verbal reminders.

Happy to set up a free pilot for one location if you want to test it.

## First 10 Customers Plan
### Goal
Get 10 real operator conversations, not 10 fantasy signups.

### Step 1 — Build demo assets
- One realistic demo restaurant account
- One default opening checklist
- 45-second walkthrough video
- 3 screenshots: checklist view, photo proof, manager dashboard

### Step 2 — First outreach batch
Reach out to:
- 30 local restaurant owners/managers
- 10 food trucks
- 10 small multi-location operators

### Step 3 — Offer setup, not software
Say:
- "I’ll set up your checklist for you"
- "You can test it free for a week"
- "Takes 15 minutes"

### Step 4 — Learn what they actually care about
Ask each pilot:
- What tasks get skipped most often?
- What do managers have to double-check manually?
- What usually goes wrong before service?
- Is inspection prep or shift readiness the bigger pain?

### Step 5 — Measure pilot quality
Track:
- Did they complete setup?
- Did staff actually use it?
- Did the manager check the dashboard?
- Which checklist items mattered most?
- Would they pay to keep it?

5. Warm intros from restaurant-adjacent people

## Content Ideas
- "5 dumb reasons restaurants fail inspections"

- "The easiest stuff to miss before service becomes the most expensive stuff later"

## Success Criteria for GTM v1
- 10 operator conversations
- 3 pilot users
- 1 paying location or strong pull to continue
- clear signal on whether the wedge is inspection prep, opening readiness, or staff accountability

## Biggest Risk
Operators may understand it instantly but still think paper is good enough. To beat that, keep tying the app to:
- inspection failures
- opening delays
- manager time wasted
- repeated skipped basics
- lack of proof/accountability
